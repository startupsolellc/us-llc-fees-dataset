Data Verification Methodology

At Formation.Legal, we believe that global founders and developers deserve access to accurate, transparent, and unbiased data when navigating the complexities of US business formation.

This page outlines the strict methodology we use to compile, verify, and maintain the US LLC Fees Dataset.

1. The Problem with "Secondary Sources"

Finding accurate state fees online is notoriously difficult. If you search for "LLC fees by state", the first page of search results is typically dominated by:

  • Registered Agent Services: These companies often bundle state fees with their own service fees, making it impossible to determine the true, underlying state cost.
  • Outdated Blogs: State legislatures update their fee schedules frequently. A blog post written in 2023 is highly likely to contain inaccurate information for 2026.
  • "Free LLC" Traps: Many services advertise "$0 LLC Formation", obscuring the fact that states still require mandatory filing fees, franchise taxes, and annual reports.

Our Rule: We never rely on blogs, third-party legal services (e.g., LegalZoom, ZenBusiness, TRUiC), or SEO articles to verify our data.

2. Primary Sources Only (.gov)

To guarantee 100% data integrity, every single data point in our states.json file is sourced exclusively from official state government websites.

When compiling data for a specific state, we rely on:

  1. Secretary of State (SOS) Portals: The primary governing body for business registrations in most states.
  2. Division of Corporations / Department of Revenue: For states that separate taxation and registration (e.g., New Jersey, Rhode Island).
  3. State Statutes & Legislative Updates: To track upcoming fee waivers or changes (e.g., Pennsylvania's shift to annual reporting in 2025).

3. The Definition of "Formation Fee"

The formation_fee in our dataset represents the base, state-mandated filing fee for standard Domestic Articles of Organization.

  • What is included: The exact cost to submit the paperwork to the state. Where states charge different fees for paper vs. online filing, we record the online filing fee, as it represents the modern standard.
  • What is NOT included: Optional expedited processing fees, third-party registered agent fees, name reservation fees, or county-level newspaper publication fees (which vary wildly in states like New York and Nebraska).

4. The Definition of "Annual Report Fee"

The annual_report_fee represents the minimum recurring cost required to keep the LLC in "Good Standing" with the state.

  • Depending on the state, this may be legally referred to as an Annual Report, Biennial Statement, Franchise Tax Minimum, or License Tax.
  • If a state has a minimum mandatory tax (like Rhode Island's $400 minimum or California's $800 franchise tax), this is factored into the recurring compliance cost for accuracy.

5. Traceability and Versioning

We believe in "trust, but verify." In our states.json dataset, every state object includes two critical fields:

  • source_url: The exact .gov link where the fee was verified.
  • last_verified: The YYYY-MM-DD timestamp of when our team last checked the data against the official source.

By strictly adhering to this methodology, the US LLC Fees Dataset serves as a reliable, single source of truth for developers building calculators, researchers analyzing state economies, and entrepreneurs utilizing tools like our 3-Year LLC Cost Calculator.
